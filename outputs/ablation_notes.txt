========================================
BINARY MAZE - ABLATION
========================================

1. REPRESENTATIONS
------------------

NARROW REPRESENTATION
- Edits one tile at a time in a fixed sequence.
- Action space is small (only tile value change: 0 or 1).
- Provides controlled and stable updates.

Behavior:
- Learns faster (high sample efficiency).
- Produces consistent and stable results.
- Strong performance even at low training steps.

Conclusion:
Narrow representation simplifies the learning problem by restricting the action space, leading to faster convergence and higher stability.


WIDE REPRESENTATION
- Can modify any tile at any time.
- Action space is large (grid_size² × tile values).
- Provides full flexibility in editing.

Behavior:
- Slower learning due to large search space.
- Higher variance across runs.
- Improves gradually with more training steps.

Conclusion:
Wide representation enables more flexibility but increases optimization difficulty, requiring more training to achieve comparable performance.


----------------------------------------

2. METRICS USED
---------------

avg_reward:
- Mean total reward across episodes.
- Indicates how well the agent optimizes the reward function.

std_reward:
- Variance of reward across seeds.
- Measures training stability.

success_rate:
- Percentage of generated levels that satisfy the success condition (valid path).
- Main performance metric.

std_success:
- Variance in success across seeds.
- Indicates consistency of learning.

avg_path_length:
- Average length of valid path from start to goal.
- Measures quality of generated solutions.

avg_connectivity:
- Fraction of connected empty tiles.
- Measures global structure of the level.

avg_empty_ratio:
- Proportion of empty tiles in the grid.
- Measures density balance.

seed_rewards / seed_success / seed_paths:
- Raw per-seed values.
- Used to analyze variance and reproducibility.

3. KEY OBSERVATIONS FROM RESULTS
--------------------------------

NARROW vs WIDE (EARLY TRAINING)
- Narrow achieves high success quickly (~0.85–0.90 at 50k steps).
- Wide performs poorly initially (~0.20 at 50k steps).

Conclusion:
Narrow representation is significantly more sample-efficient.


TRAINING PROGRESSION
- Narrow shows strong early performance with minor fluctuations.
- Wide improves steadily with more training steps.

Conclusion:
Wide representation benefits from longer training but lags behind narrow in early stages.


CONNECTIVITY RELATIONSHIP
- Higher connectivity strongly correlates with higher success.
- Wide representation shows low connectivity early, improving over time.

Conclusion:
Connectivity is a key driver of success.


PATH LENGTH CONSISTENCY
- Path length remains nearly constant (~18) across all experiments.

Conclusion:
Once a valid solution is found, both representations produce similar quality paths.
The main challenge is finding valid solutions, not optimizing them.


ENTROPY EFFECT
- Higher entropy (0.05) reduces success rate for both representations.
- Leads to increased instability.

Conclusion:
Excessive exploration negatively impacts structured learning.


----------------------------------------

4. ABLATION INSIGHTS
--------------------

NO_CONNECTIVITY
- Removes global connectivity reward.

Effect:
- Significant drop in success rate.
- Disconnected maps.

Conclusion:
Connectivity is the most critical reward component.


NO_CORRIDOR
- Removes corridor/maze structure reward.

Effect:
- Minimal change in performance.

Conclusion:
Corridor structure has low importance.
